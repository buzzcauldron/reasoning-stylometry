We can specify a curve α in the 1-skeleton of ∂R K which is parallel to the core K whose projection runs just to the right of K in the projection plane. Choose an orientation of K. A longitude of a knot K in R3 is characterized by having a linking number with K equal to zero. The linking number is easily calculated from the pro jection. To each crossing of K is associated a sign, and the sum of these signs, called the writhe of the knot, gives the linking number of a curve with K. To ensure that the linking number is zero, we alter the construction of α . Near every overcrossing of K, we add a full twist of α around K. This twist is positive if the sign of the crossing is negative, and vice versa. The result is a knot K2 in R3 that lies on the peripheral torus whose linking number with K is zero. We can construct the associated annulus S in RK with boundary components K and K2 similar to the proof given for Theorem 4.1, and it has O(n2) triangles.
