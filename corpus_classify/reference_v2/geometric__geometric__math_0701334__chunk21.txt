Intersection theorems Our methods enable us to deduce somewhat surprising results concerning intersections of the form ∩k i=1wi(G), where w1,...,w k are given words. Note that in free groups such intersection may well be trivial. Ho wever, we can prove that in the groups considered in this paper such inters ections are very large. More precisley, we have Theorem 8.1. Letk ≥ 1 and let w1,...,w k be non-trivial words. (i) There exists N =N (w1,...,w k) such that for all n ≥ N we have (w1(An) ∩ ... ∩ wk(An))2 =An; (ii) For every ǫ> 0 there exists N =N (w1,...,w k,ǫ) such that |w1(An) ∩ ... ∩ wk(An)| ≥ n− 4−ǫ|An|. Indeed, part (i) follows from Theorems 1.1 and 1.3, using the fact that, for n ≫ 0, σSn n ⊆ ∩ k i=1wi(An). The proof of part (ii) is similar to that of Theorem 1.9. Our next result is an intersection theorem for groups of Lie t ype. Theorem 8.2. Letk ≥ 1 and let w1,...,w k be non-trivial words. Let G a simply connected almost simple algebraic group over a ﬁnite ﬁeld Fq. (i) There is a positive constant c depending only on w1,...,w k and dimG such that |w1(G(Fq)) ∩ ... ∩ wk(G(Fq))| ≥ c|G(Fq)|. (iii) There is a number N depending only on w1,...,w k and dimG such that, if Γ = G(Fq)/Z (G(Fq)) is a ﬁnite simple group associated with G, and |Γ| ≥ N , then (w1(Γ) ∩ ... ∩ wk(Γ))2 = Γ.
