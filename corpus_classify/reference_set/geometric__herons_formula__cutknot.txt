Heron's formula is named after Hero of Alexandria. The formula is a specialization of Brahmagupta's formula for cyclic quadrilaterals, since, after all, every triangle is a cyclic quadrilateral with two coalesced vertices. The proof below exploits the dissection of a triangle induced by the presence of the incircle.

In triangle ABC, the lengths of the segments from the vertices to the points of tangency of the incircle are found to be

x = s − a, y = s − b, z = s − c,

so that Heron's formula can also be written as S² = sxyz.

Let r be the inradius of triangle ABC. The rearrangement of the six triangles formed by this dissection shows immediately that

S = rs.

Let I be the incenter and denote w = AI. From the resulting diagram,

xyz = r²(x + y + z) = r²s.

It then follows that sxyz = r²s² = S², which completes the proof.
