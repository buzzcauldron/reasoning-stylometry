This proof appears in the Book IV of Mathematical Collection by Pappus of Alexandria (ca A.D. 300) [ Eves , Pappas ]. It generalizes the Pythagorean Theorem in two ways: the triangle ABC is not required to be right-angled and
the shapes built on its sides are arbitrary parallelograms instead of squares. Thus build parallelograms CADE and CBFG on sides AC and, respectively, BC. Let DE and FG meet in H and draw AL and BM parallel and equal to HC. Then Area(ABML) = Area(CADE) + Area(CBFG). Indeed, with the sheering transformation already used in proofs #1 and #12, Area(CADE) = Area(CAUH) = Area(SLAR) and also Area(CBFG) = Area(CBVH) = Area(SMBR). Now, just add up what's equal.
