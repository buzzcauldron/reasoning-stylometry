The main purpose of this paper is to prove an even stronger res ult for certain families of ﬁnite simple groups, where the exponent 3 is replaced by 2 (see Theorems 1.5 and 1.6 below). Along the way we prove seve ral results of independent interest, related to the size of w(G), and to powers of certain conjugacy classes C in G. Given a conjugacy class C ̸= 1 in a ﬁnite simple group G there exists a number k such that Ck = G. Substantial work has been devoted to the study of these numbers k, and related so-called covering numbers (see e.g. [AH, EGH, LL, LiSh]). A particular challenge is to show that C 2 =G in certain cases. Indeed a conjecture of Thompson states that every ﬁnite simple group G has a class C with this property. In spite of considerable progress this i s still open in general. Now let C = σSn be a conjugacy class in Sn. When can we say that C 2 = An? This problem has quite a long history.
