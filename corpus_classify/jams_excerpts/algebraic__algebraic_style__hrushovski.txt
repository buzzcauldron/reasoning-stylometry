We will need some lemmas on finite generation. First, if E is a finitely generated group, N a normal subgroup with E modulo N finitely presented, then N is finitely generated as a normal subgroup. In particular when E modulo N is finite, this implies the finite generation of N, a well-known statement used above. This in fact valid for any equational class: If E is finitely generated and N is a congruence with E modulo N finitely presented, then N is finitely generated as a congruence. Indeed let F be a finitely generated free algebra in this equational class, and h from F to E a surjective homomorphism. Let g from F to E modulo N be the composition F to E to E modulo N. Since E modulo N is finitely presented, g has a finitely generated kernel K. Thus N equals h of K is finitely generated.
